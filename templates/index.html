About

Bike shares are becoming increasingly common in cities in the United States. Commuters have been using bike sharing as a method of transportation for the flexibility, cost savings, exercise, and a myriad of other benefits. While being quite successful, many bike shares are still relatively new and have room for optimization.

Regular vs. Non-regular Riders

All pass (flex, monthly) holders are considered to be regular bike share riders, and thus there is a total of 90821 regular users, which is 68.6% of all bike share users.

Top 10 Most Popular Starting Stations

The top popular starting stations are computed by the number of trips recorded with the corresponding station id. The top 10 popular starting stations with the number of trips are graphed above, and the most poopular starting station is 3069.

The Top 10 Most Popular Ending Stations

The top popular ending stations are computed by the number of trips recorded with the corresponding station id. The top 10 popular ending stations with the number of trips are graphed above, and the most poopular ending station is 3005.

Distance Distribution

The distance distributation is calculated by rounding the distance, in miles, to the tenth digit. The average distance traveled is 1.51 miles.

Distance Distribution

The bar graph shows the relationship between trip route (one-way/round trip) and pass type. It is important to note that monthly passholders have the greatest number of one-way trips, while walk-up passholders have the greatest number of round-trips.
